1. Objective.¶

The University of the Pacific have arranged "ALL-YOU-CAN-EAT" buffet and wish to have all the students at this buffet. So University have decide to run a coupn campaigns that sends targeted emails to the students which are likely to encourage them to come. The university has hired me to create a recommendation engine for email coupon campaigns.

To create an engine, a detailed analysis of the dataset needs to be carried out. This dataset includes information on food choices, nutrition, preferences, childhood favorites, and other information from college students. Data is raw and uncleaned. It might require lot of Cleaning in the process.

This analysis will help the university to design the coupon for the campaign "ALL-YOU-CAN-EAT". Each cluster will be given a seperate distinguish coupon. For this a dataset containing responses from 126 students is given to perform the analysis. The aim of this analysis is to predict the clusters of the new students so that they can be provided with the most appropriate coupon that matches their interests.

def kmeans(centroid1,centroid2,centroid3,food,k):
    food["Dist_C1"] = np.sqrt((food.thai_food - centroid1[0])**2 
                             + (food.ethnic_food - centroid1[1])**2+(food.cook - centroid1[2])**2)
    food["Dist_C2"] = np.sqrt((food.thai_food - centroid2[0])**2 
                             + (food.ethnic_food - centroid2[1])**2+(food.cook - centroid2[2])**2)
    food["Dist_C3"] = np.sqrt((food.thai_food - centroid3[0])**2 
                             + (food.ethnic_food - centroid3[1])**2+(food.cook - centroid3[2])**2)
###
    food["Association"] = np.where(
    (food.Dist_C1 < food.Dist_C2) & 
    (food.Dist_C1 < food.Dist_C3), 1, 
    np.where((food.Dist_C2 < food.Dist_C1) & 
    (food.Dist_C2 < food.Dist_C3) , 2, 3))

###
   # food[food.Association == 1]
    #food[food.Association == 2]
    #food[food.Association == 3].head(1)

    centroi1 = food[food.Association == 1][["thai_food","ethnic_food","cook"]].mean()
    centroi2 = food[food.Association == 2][["thai_food","ethnic_food","cook"]].mean()
    centroi3 = food[food.Association == 3][["thai_food","ethnic_food","cook"]].mean()
    
    forcluster1=food[food.Association == 1]
    cluster_1 = forcluster1['studentid'].values
    cluster_1
    
    forcluster2=food[food.Association == 2]
    cluster_2 = forcluster2['studentid'].values
    cluster_2
    
    forcluster3=food[food.Association == 3]
    cluster_3 = forcluster3['studentid'].values
    cluster_3
  
    return(centroi1,centroi2,centroi3,cluster_1,cluster_2,cluster_3)

A kmeans() function is created that accepts a "dataframe" and "k" as parameters, and returns the clusters created.

8. Summary(Recommendations)¶

A dataset was selected, which contains responses from 126 students about their interests and other related information about their food preferences. The University wants to build a coupon engine that sends the targeted coupons to the students to motivate them to come to the All-YOU-CAN-EAT buffet.

From all the responses the one variable which had a stronger relationship with any of the other variable is most certain to predict the interest of the other students. So a correlation matrix is prepared to find out the strongest relation among all the variables. Three variables "cook", "thai_food", "ethnic_food" were selected as they showing a strong relationship with other variables. The student who likes Thai food also likes ethnic food and the person who cooks often might like home-made-food so such information can be useful in making coupons.

Those who do some kind of sports activity will generally prefer healthy food if given an option.
